Who am I?

I have been a geographer with a focus on using geospatial tools for environmental conservation for the past 5 years. Most of my time has been spent with the Urban Ecology Center where community engagement around nature was prevelent. I love to learn, to help and share with others. I am incredibly passionate, knowledgeable and interested in birds and bird conservation. I have helped Wisconsin eBird identify underbirded areas in Wisconin in addition to leading weekly bird walks for over 3 years at the Urban Ecology Center. I have a strong understanding of natural systems with an emphasis on bird identification.

Additionally, I am committed to creating equitable spaces for BIPOC communities in the outdoors. During my time at the UEC, I held a leadership role on the Equity, Dignity and Justice Committee, where I facilitated conversations and action that ensured our organization was a truly equitable and dignifying organization. I helped spearhead an anti-racism public statement following the murder of George Floyd and worked continuously to create action-based change to push the UEC to become an anti-racist organization. I currently on the leadership team for the Urban Bird Collective in Minneapolis whose goal is to create safe and equitable spaces for LGBTQ and BIPOC folks to gather and bird watch outside.
